Team Canvas - Leveraging the power of a canvas for team improvements

v0.4

Motivation

Products should be developed strategically. If that is not the case than it is not product development but only fire fighting. For teams the same principle is true. In order to move from fire fighting to strategic team development typical retrospective tools are not enough.

The Canvas is a good fit for this purpose as it is adaptable, visible and easy to use.

Introduction to the "Canvas" tool

The original Canvas is the Business Model Canvas helping enterprises to summarize their business. This concept was further enhanced and reused for product development in the Product Canvas. Additionally the Canvas is starting to gain grounds for change management. An example is the Lean Change Canvas. Keep in mind that team development also needs change management if done effectively.

Team Canvas

Composition

Our vision

How should the team be defined?

This is the team's vision how they would describe themselves in the future with only one short sentence. I strongly suggest that the team comes up with one. Take a look at the book Coaching Agile Teams and the chapter "Create a Shared Vision" if you need ideas how to let the team come up with a vision.

Target state

How does the future look like?

The target state describes the environment and traits the team has, when the vision is met. Examples:

  • Won Sprints are the norm
  • New products are pro-actively identified
  • A wide span of technologies is used

Urgent

What hurts currently the most?

This is the place you can attach typical impediments happening during the Sprint. At the retrospective the team can decide if this is something which needs to be tackled "strategically" or not.

Actions

What is done in order to reach the target state?

Improvements are put up here. They should always have a connection to the target state. If this is not the case you should check if you need to adjust the target state or scrap the improvement. Examples:

  • Introduce 10 % free allocatable time for every dev team member so they can choose what they are working on. Supports target state item "New products are pro-actively identified"

Success criteria

How to check if the action is a success?

Here starts the fun part. It is often very difficult to come up with a good type of measurement. If the improvement should reap only long-term benefits which can be measured, consider slicing the action or keeping the action on the board until verified/falsified. Examples:

  • Two feasible product ideas reach the Product Owner every month. Check for "Introduce 10 % free allocatable time for every dev team member"

Verified improvements

What action is a success?

A place to put successful improvements. The goal is to have a hall of achievements and a reminder how the "process" looks like. There is no room for non-working improvements on purpose as this would invite exclamations like "It did not work before, why should it now?".

Required investment

What is needed to implement the action?

This is the place to have a discussion on "resources":

  • How many "Man Days" will be burned to implement the action?
  • Do we need to buy additional hardware?
  • ...
"There's no such thing as a free lunch." This often gets overlooked. This section is there to prevent that and make discussions with the supporting management or other departments easier.

Communication

Who do we need to implement the action?

If you need "Bob" from the Devops team to tweak your CI environment, than you probably should have a talk with him (and his manager) ;-). In that case there would be a post-it in this section with Bob (and Bob's manager).

Action champions

Who is driving the action forward?

Somebody needs to feel responsible to drive the action forward. Keep in mind that multiple people should not be responsible for the same action. If you are familiar with the RACI matrix, this person would be "Responsible".

Impacted people

Who will feel the impact of the implemented action?

This sounds very similar to the "Communication" section. The "Communication" section is about the people needed for the output. The "Impacted people" section is about the people affected by the outcome. Keep in mind that actions do not only need to have positive outcomes for everyone. Put the negative ones up here too. This will make it much easier to "sell the idea".

Addons

Sheet for ad-hoc ideas

During the iteration the team might come up with good ideas for improvements. If you attach a sheet of paper right next to the Team Canvas the team members can add new possible improvements whenever they like. In the retrospective check those ideas and flesh them out.

Field test

The idea was tested with one team.

Conclusion

The result of the field test are quite promising. The Team Canvas really seems to help to have an overview of the improvements. If used properly by the Agile Coach/ScrumMaster/Team it really helps starting important discussions.
